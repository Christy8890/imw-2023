Interaction Process

By: Christy Wang

1. Resize interaction

My assigned interaction was resizing!!! Was pretty nervous when I found out I got this one. Not sure what to do with this yet

2. Visual research

I found white box website very inspirational, this never ending resizing of squares. I wonder if I can do something similar. Block puzzle game on the order hand doesn’t involve the users resizing anything, but I could potentially add that as a new feature

3. Research on resizing code

Before I jump into the prototype, I just wanted to try out using this feature. Not only did I tried out coding it, but I also explore a basic design of the resize feature, which is the corner design, that we normally see on the web.

4. 1st prototype attempt

My first few sets prototype involves creating a puzzle game. Users can resize the puzzle pieces and put it together to reveal the object. I like the 3rd one the most. It has a template of the final shape already int he background and the user will just have to find the correct pieces within a pile of wrong puzzle pieces

5. Feedback from Peter

During my feedback session with Peter, I showed him the puzzle game prototype that I created. However, I did felt like it was too surface level. Peter advised me to dig deeper into “resize” and suggest me to ask more questions about it.

I realized here that I never went and research the history behind this feature and why it’s an important interaction within the digital space. I explored...what if this feature disappeared for a day? How would that effect user’s overall experience? What platforms use this feature the most?

I found out a lot of facts I didn’t know about this feature and I was inspired by it.

6. Back to visual research

After attempting to re-brainstorm my ideas, I went back to visual researching. I grab some inspirations from existing work. I tried to explore beyond puzzles and looked at cropping and scaling as well.

7. Ideation exploration

Then I came up with a few ideas based on what I like from the research

8. 2nd low-fi wireframe attempt

After my chat with Peter, I went back to my inspo board and started brainstorming and trying to properly frame the problem this time. One of my feedback was that there wasn’t enough contrast, so I looked for a colour palette that’s dark mode + a bright colour. I also wanted my poster to have hook at the beginning to grab the users attention just like the some these example have.

9. Back to low-fi ireframes

Now back to the wireframes once again.....I made sure to add a hook and explore columns as suggested from my feedback

10. Back to hi-fi wireframes

Then, during my iterations in the hi-fi wireframes phase, you can tell that I added more and more spaces to each of the sections to create a flow rather than showing everything right when the user land on the page

11. Coding attempt I

This is my first draft that I coded, I like how I started off by introducing a hook to grab the user’s attention and then slowly revealed more info as they scroll down. I also made sure that I have some sort of CTA button in every section.

12. Coding Attempt II

After finsihing coding the foundation of the poster, I added hovering interactions to the speaker series and the title of the conference.

13. Coding Attempt III

In my final draft, I iterate and explored the typography a little bit more. For the hook, I tried underlining, highlighting, italicizing, and typing animation to enhance the hierarchy. Plus, I felt like it was a bit flat w/ just the green highlight in my first draft.

I also added italics to the web on the title to match the top. I think it made a big difference.

I did the same with the “Save the Date” section. One of my favourite iteration was adding “Add to Calendar” button. This will bring users directly to their Google Calendar!

14. Reflection!

Looking back, I’m happy that I went out of my way to explore different types of design layouts. I feel like if I stick to the first draft I made I wouldn’t have been satisfied with my work at the end. I learned a lot about what I like and dislike as a design and lessons about contrast (accessibility) and the importance of understanding the user experience from Peter Ha.

I especially liked how I turn the initial static poster to an interactive digital poster that is able to bring users in a journey to explore the conference. Hooking the users from the moment they land on the page, then leading through the what the conference offers and lastly, encouraging the users to take action and register for the conference at the end.
