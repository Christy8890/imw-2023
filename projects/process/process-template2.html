Poster Process

By: Christy Wang

1. Brainstorming

After choosing my the topic, I started off by brainstorming and asking myself about the user. What does my target user want to gain from my posters? And then asked myself about CTA, what actions do I want my users to take after viewing this?

2. Visual Research

Then I gather a bunch of inspo from Pinterest & examples from the lecture. I was very inspired by the thin lines, neon colours and the decorative typefaces.

3. Lo-Fi Wireframes I

My first set of wire frames felt more like a traditional poster rather than an interactive one. The type seemed way to big anf I did take inconsideration of the interactiveness

4. Lo-Fi Wireframes II

My next set of wireframes felt a bit better....I explore more with the placement and that was pretty much it

5. UI Elements

I quickly jumped into creating a UI palette consisting of primary colours and typography. I liked the settle cream with a pop of vibrant blue. Roboto Mono reminds me on coding and web, so I decided to pair that up with Inter.

6. Hi-Fi Wireframes Attempt I

Then I went ahead with adding the colours to the posters. Looking back, it is hard to read with the low contrast and it felt like it was too much

7. Feedback from Peter Ha

Before I started coding, I booked a 1-1 with Peter Ha for some feedback. Here is where i realized that I didn’t really consider about the user experience aspect of it. Didn’t know exactly who’s going to looking at this, what will hook them in, what are they looking for and what do they need...ect

8. Back to visual research

After my chat with Peter, I went back to my inspo board and started brainstorming and trying to properly frame the problem this time. One of my feedback was that there wasn’t enough contrast, so I looked for a colour palette that’s dark mode + a bright colour. I also wanted my poster to have hook at the beginning to grab the users attention just like the some these example have.

9. Back to low-fi ireframes

Now back to the wireframes once again.....I made sure to add a hook and explore columns as suggested from my feedback

10. Back to hi-fi wireframes

Then, during my iterations in the hi-fi wireframes phase, you can tell that I added more and more spaces to each of the sections to create a flow rather than showing everything right when the user land on the page

11. Coding attempt I

This is my first draft that I coded, I like how I started off by introducing a hook to grab the user’s attention and then slowly revealed more info as they scroll down. I also made sure that I have some sort of CTA button in every section.

12. Coding Attempt II

After finsihing coding the foundation of the poster, I added hovering interactions to the speaker series and the title of the conference.

13. Reflection!

Looking back, I’m happy that I went out of my way to explore different types of design layouts. I feel like if I stick to the first draft I made I wouldn’t have been satisfied with my work at the end. I learned a lot about what I like and dislike as a design and lessons about contrast (accessibility) and the importance of understanding the user experience from Peter Ha.

I especially liked how I turn the initial static poster to an interactive digital poster that is able to bring users in a journey to explore the conference. Hooking the users from the moment they land on the page, then leading through the what the conference offers and lastly, encouraging the users to take action and register for the conference at the end.
